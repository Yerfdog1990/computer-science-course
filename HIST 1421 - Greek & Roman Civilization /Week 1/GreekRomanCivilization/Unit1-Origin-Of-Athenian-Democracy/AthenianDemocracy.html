Who Were "The People"?

Participation in Athenian democracy was surprisingly limited. This section explores the demographics of Attica and what it meant to be a citizen with political rights.

The Demographics of Power

Population estimates for Attica in the 4th century BCE. Note the small fraction of enfranchised citizens.

The Athenian Franchise

Political rights were restricted to free adult male citizens over 18. This excluded a vast majority of the population, including women, resident aliens (metics), and the large enslaved population. In 451 BCE, Pericles' law further restricted citizenship, requiring both parents to be citizens. This meant political power was held by only 10-20% of the total population.

Loss of Rights (Atimia)

A citizen could be disfranchised for offenses like debt to the treasury, cowardice in battle, or failing to support his parents.

Holding Power Accountable
